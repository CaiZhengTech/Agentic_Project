# Contacting sales and pre-sales questions

Support handles existing-account issues; for anything about choosing a plan, custom pricing,
or pre-purchase questions, sales is the right team.

## When to talk to sales instead of support

- You haven't signed up yet and want to compare plans for your team's needs.
- You need a custom Enterprise arrangement (e.g., split-tier seats, custom integrations
  scoping, volume discounts for 50+ seats).
- You want a live demo before committing.
- You're evaluating Northbeam against a competitor and have specific requirements to
  validate.
- You need a signed contract, MSA, or procurement paperwork (common for larger
  organizations) rather than self-service signup.

## How to reach sales

- Fill out the form at northbeamit.com/contact-sales with your company size and rough
  requirements — a rep typically responds within 1 business day.
- Existing customers can also request a sales callback from Account → Billing → Talk to
  Sales, useful when discussing an upgrade path that isn't a simple self-service change
  (e.g., custom Enterprise terms).

## What sales can help with that support cannot

Support can tell you what your *current* plan includes and troubleshoot issues within it.
Support cannot negotiate custom pricing, draft contracts, or commit to features outside the
standard plan catalog — those go through sales.

## When to contact support instead

If your question is about an existing account (billing on your current plan, a feature not
working, technical troubleshooting), that's support, not sales — see the relevant KB doc for
your issue, or open a support ticket directly rather than waiting on a sales response.
