# Resetting your password and unlocking your account

If you've forgotten your password or your account has been locked after too many failed
login attempts, follow these steps.

## Resetting a forgotten password

1. On the Northbeam login page, click "Forgot password?" and enter your account email.
2. You'll receive a reset link by email within a few minutes. Check your spam/junk folder if
   it doesn't arrive — some corporate mail filters delay or quarantine automated mail.
3. The link expires after 30 minutes. If it expires, request a new one; only the most recent
   link is valid.
4. Choose a new password (minimum 12 characters, at least one number and one symbol). You'll
   be logged out of all other active sessions for security.

## Unlocking a locked account

Accounts lock automatically after 5 consecutive failed login attempts, for 15 minutes.

1. Wait 15 minutes and try again with the correct password — most lockouts clear
   automatically.
2. If you don't remember your password, use "Forgot password?" above rather than continuing
   to guess; repeated failed attempts extend the lockout window.
3. If the account is still locked after 15 minutes and a correct password, your account may
   have been manually locked (e.g., after a suspected security concern — see "Reporting a
   security concern"). This requires support to clear.

## Multi-factor authentication issues

If you've lost access to your MFA device (phone, authenticator app), you cannot self-service
reset MFA for security reasons. Contact support with your account email and be prepared to
verify your identity through an alternate method (e.g., a manager confirmation for corporate
accounts).

## When to contact support

Contact support if: a password reset link never arrives after checking spam and waiting 15
minutes, your account remains locked after the 15-minute window with correct credentials, or
you've lost your MFA device. Include your account email and roughly when the issue started.
