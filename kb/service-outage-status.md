# Checking service status and planned maintenance

Before opening a ticket for a service issue, check whether it's a known, already-tracked
problem.

## Status page

The Northbeam status page (status.northbeamit.com) shows real-time status for all major
services: VPN, email, API, and the customer portal. Each incident includes a description,
start time, affected regions, and current status (investigating / identified / monitoring /
resolved).

- Subscribe to updates for a specific incident by clicking "Subscribe" — you'll get an email
  when status changes, no need to keep refreshing.
- Historical incidents (past 90 days) are listed at the bottom of the page if you want to
  confirm something you experienced was a known issue.

## Planned maintenance

Scheduled maintenance windows are posted on the status page at least 48 hours in advance,
with expected start time, duration, and affected services. We schedule maintenance outside
core business hours (typically 1–4am ET) whenever possible.

- During maintenance, affected services may be briefly unavailable or degraded — this is
  expected and does not need a ticket.
- If a maintenance window runs longer than its posted duration, the status page will be
  updated with a new estimate.

## If the status page shows "all systems operational" but you're still having issues

This means the problem is likely isolated to your account, device, or network rather than a
broader outage. Check the relevant troubleshooting doc for your issue (e.g., "Troubleshooting
VPN connection drops," "Setting up your email client") before contacting support.

## When to contact support

Contact support if: the status page shows an active incident matching your issue and it's
been unresolved for longer than the posted estimate, or if you're experiencing a service
problem the status page doesn't reflect at all (this helps us catch issues faster).
